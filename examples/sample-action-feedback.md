# Action Feedback: Homepage Headline Draft — Example Local Website Project

> This is a **fictional, public-safe example** of an Action Feedback record. It shows how to capture what actually happened after acting on an AI task. No real customer or private data is included, and no success metrics are invented. See the [Action Feedback template](../templates/Action_Feedback_Template.md) for a blank version.

## Action attempted

Asked an AI tool for three homepage headline options for the fictional bakery site, using the Context Pack that pointed to the Project Map, the tone notes, and the relevant decisions.

## Expected result

Three on-tone, ready-to-use headlines, with one good enough to use as the primary headline without edits.

## Actual result

Three headlines were returned and all matched the warm, local tone. Two were usable as written. The third drifted toward a marketing cliché and needed to be dropped. One small edit was made to the chosen headline to shorten it.

## What worked

- Pointing the AI at the tone notes kept the voice consistent.
- Listing the decisions to respect prevented suggestions that assumed a backend or a CMS.
- The length constraint kept the options short and scannable.

## What failed

- The "out of scope" list didn't explicitly forbid clichés, so one option slipped in a tired phrase.
- The Context Pack didn't say which page section the headline sits above, so one option assumed a hero image that may not exist.

## Reusable lesson

When briefing an AI for copy, name the clichés and phrases to avoid, and state where the text will appear. Specific "don'ts" and placement context reduce off-tone output.
