**Summary:** How to keep website copy on-tone for a small, local business — and how to brief an AI so its drafts match that tone.

## The tone

For the Example Local Website Project (a fictional local bakery), site copy should feel:

- **Warm** — friendly and human, not corporate.
- **Simple** — short sentences, plain words, no jargon.
- **Local** — speaks to neighbours and nearby visitors.
- **Trustworthy** — calm and honest, no hype or pressure.

## What to avoid

- Marketing clichés ("best in town", "world-class", "game-changing").
- Invented claims — no awards, prices, or statistics that aren't true.
- Exclamation-heavy or salesy phrasing.

## How to apply it (including with AI)

When asking an AI to draft copy:

1. State the tone words above **and** the phrases to avoid — naming the "don'ts" matters as much as the tone.
2. Say **where** the text appears (e.g. homepage hero, contact page intro) so the draft fits its placement.
3. Give an explicit length limit when space is tight.
4. Forbid invented facts about the business.
