## 5. Long book description (~350–500 words)

**Your AI keeps forgetting — and it's costing you time.**

AI assistants like Claude, Codex, ChatGPT, and local models are genuinely useful, but they share one frustrating limit: they forget. Every new session starts cold. You re-paste the same background, re-explain the same goals, and still get answers that ignore decisions you already made. Bigger context windows don't fix it — the real problem is that your project memory lives nowhere reliable.

**Chat history is not project memory.** A transcript is hard to search, easy to lose, locked to one vendor, and structured for conversation rather than reuse. Scroll back far enough and even *you* can't reconstruct why a choice was made.

**And ordinary notes aren't enough for AI agents.** Most notes are organized for a human to browse, not for a model to act on. An AI doesn't just need your notes — it needs to know what each item *is* and where to start.

*Build Your Local AI Knowledge OS* is a practical field guide to solving this with something refreshingly simple: plain Markdown files, organized by meaning and role, that any AI tool can read.

**You'll build a small, owned system** with a clear structure — capture, distilling, durable knowledge, an index, your active projects, and a dedicated area of rules for AI tools. On top of it you'll use a handful of copyable templates:

- **Project Maps** — a living front door for every project.
- **Decision Records** — capture *why* a choice was made.
- **Incident Records** — learn from what went wrong.
- **Action Feedback** — record what actually happened, so the system improves.
- **Context Packs** — focused briefs that give an AI exactly what it needs for one task.

**A free public companion repository** provides the starter kit, templates, diagrams, and fictional, public-safe examples so you can copy the structure and start the same day. The full book manuscript stays private; the repo is a companion resource, not the book.

**You'll learn how to** set up the system, capture and distill knowledge, run AI tasks from focused context, close the feedback loop, and use the same structure across Claude, Codex, ChatGPT, and local LLMs — staying tool-agnostic and local-first throughout.

**Who it's for:** solo founders, AI consultants, indie hackers, technical writers, Markdown and Obsidian users, AI power users, and small teams that need reusable project memory.

**A note on safety:** this is a local-first method. Your real work stays in your own local or private workspace; only public-safe templates and examples are shared. The book makes no income, automation, or guaranteed-outcome claims — just a clear, practical system you can build and own.

## 12. Sales page warnings and boundaries

The sales copy (everywhere — KDP listing, A+ content, launch posts) must avoid:

- **No guaranteed income claims** — never imply the book earns the reader money.
- **No exaggerated automation claims** — this is a memory method, not an automation engine.
- **No fake metrics** — no invented stats, results, or success rates.
- **No "10x" hype** — no inflated multiplier or revolution language.
- **No private customer case claims** — unless fully anonymized, public-safe, and approved.
- **No professional-advice substitution** — it does not replace professional security, legal, or business judgment.
- **No implication that the public repo contains a full private workflow** — the repo holds public-safe templates and examples only; real workflows and the manuscript stay private.

When in doubt, choose the calmer, more honest phrasing. Credibility is the selling point.
