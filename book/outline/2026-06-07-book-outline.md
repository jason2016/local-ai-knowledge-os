# Book Outline — Build Your Local AI Knowledge OS (2026-06-07)

> A practical KDP book that teaches readers to build a local-first, AI-readable knowledge system so any AI tool can pick up their projects with full context.
>
> **Note:** This GitHub repository is a **companion resource** — it provides the starter kit, templates, and safe examples referenced throughout the book. It is **not** the full manuscript. The complete book content remains private and is available through the published KDP edition.

---

## Book summary

Most people use AI assistants as goldfish: every conversation starts from scratch. This book shows how to give your AI a durable, external memory you control — a **Local AI Knowledge OS** built from plain Markdown files — so Claude, Codex, ChatGPT, and local models can all work from the same up-to-date context. It is hands-on, tool-agnostic, and built around the **Semantic OS Method**.

**Who it's for:** builders, freelancers, consultants, small teams, and serious knowledge workers who already use AI and want it to stop forgetting.

---

## Part I — Why Your AI Keeps Forgetting

**Chapter 1 — The Context Problem**
Why AI tools lose project context, why bigger context windows don't solve it, and the real cost of re-explaining everything.

**Chapter 2 — Memory You Control**
The case for local-first, plain-Markdown, tool-agnostic knowledge. Why owning your files beats locking memory inside one app or vendor.

**Chapter 3 — Meet the Semantic OS Method**
The core idea: organize by *meaning and role*, not by app or topic, so both humans and machines can reason about your knowledge.

---

## Part II — Building the Foundation

**Chapter 4 — The Folder Structure**
Walk through the OS layout: Inbox, Distill, Knowledge, Index, Projects, AI System. What each part is for and why the numbering matters. *(Companion: `starter-kit/`)*

**Chapter 5 — Capture and Distill**
Frictionless capture into the Inbox; turning raw input into meaning in Distill. Building a sustainable processing rhythm.

**Chapter 6 — Durable Knowledge and the Index**
Writing atomic, linkable notes that AI can use as grounding. Building an Index that makes everything findable.

---

## Part III — Working on Real Projects

**Chapter 7 — The Project Map**
The living front door to every project: goal, status, decisions, open questions, next actions, reusable knowledge. *(Companion: `templates/Project_Map_Template.md`, `examples/sample-project-map.md`)*

**Chapter 8 — Decisions and Incidents**
Capturing *why* with Decision Records and learning from failure with Incident Records. *(Companion: `templates/Decision_Record_Template.md`, `templates/Incident_Record_Template.md`)*

**Chapter 9 — Closing the Loop with Action Feedback**
Recording what actually happened so the OS gets smarter over time, not just bigger. *(Companion: `templates/Action_Feedback_Template.md`)*

---

## Part IV — Putting AI to Work

**Chapter 10 — The AI System Folder**
Writing system rules, reading orders, reusable prompts, and guardrails that make the OS work with any model. *(Companion: `starter-kit/10_AI_OS/09_AI_System/`)*

**Chapter 11 — Context Packs**
Assembling focused bundles so the AI gets exactly what it needs — grounded answers within context limits. *(Companion: `templates/Context_Pack_Template.md`)*

**Chapter 12 — One OS, Many Models**
Using the same files with Claude, Codex, ChatGPT, and local LLMs (Ollama, LM Studio). Practical workflows for each, fully offline options included.

---

## Part V — Living With Your Knowledge OS

**Chapter 13 — Habits and Maintenance**
Daily and weekly rhythms, keeping the OS lean, archiving finished projects, and harvesting reusable knowledge.

**Chapter 14 — Privacy, Safety, and Backups**
Keeping secrets out of AI-readable files, separating public from private, and backing up what matters.

**Chapter 15 — Growing the System**
Scaling from solo to small team, extending the structure, and adapting the Semantic OS Method to your own work.
