# 2026-06-07 — KDP Cover Brief: Build Your Local AI Knowledge OS

> A public-safe cover planning document for the KDP book. No private data, no manuscript text, no generated image files — prompt drafts and direction only. The full manuscript remains private.

**Book title:** Build Your Local AI Knowledge OS
**Recommended subtitle:** A Practical System for Giving AI Tools Reliable Project Memory
**Core visual positioning:** Plain-text knowledge infrastructure for serious AI users.
**Creative direction:** A calm technical field guide — not an AI startup, not a hype book, not a generic productivity book. Closer to an *operator manual* than an *AI revolution*.

| Role | Name | Hex |

- **Title / headlines:** Inter, IBM Plex Sans, Source Sans 3, or a similar clean technical sans.
- **Code / file labels:** IBM Plex Mono, JetBrains Mono, Source Code Pro, or a similar mono.
- Title must be readable at thumbnail size. Avoid futuristic / sci-fi fonts.

---

## 1. Cover objective

In three seconds, the cover must communicate:

- **This is practical** — a working system, not theory or hype.
- **This is local-first** — plain files you own, on your machine.
- **This gives AI tools reliable project memory** — the core promise.
- **This is a system the reader can build** — the title verb is "Build."
- **This is not AI hype** — calm, technical, trustworthy.

If a casual browser glances and thinks *"a serious, practical manual for using AI with my own files,"* the cover works.

---

## 2. Primary cover concept: The Field Manual Cover (Option A)

A clean technical-manual look on a warm paper background.

**Front cover layout (top to bottom):**

- **Large title** — *Build Your Local AI Knowledge OS* in a clean sans, high contrast (Graphite on Paper), occupying the upper third. Readable as a thumbnail.
- **Subtitle** — *A Practical System for Giving AI Tools Reliable Project Memory*, smaller, directly under the title.
- **Central visual** — a simplified `10_AI_OS/` folder tree rendered in mono type with thin Line-gray connectors, sitting in the middle.
- **Transformation** — the folder tree visually narrows / flows (via thin arrows) into a single **Context Pack** document on the right or below, suggesting "structure becomes usable AI context."
- **Small positioning line** near the bottom: `Local-first • Markdown-based • Tool-agnostic` in mono.
- **Overall feeling** — calm, structured, professional; lots of paper space; one clear idea.

Accent use is restrained: Ink blue for the system/folder element, Local green for the knowledge node, Soft amber only on the arrow or the Context Pack to draw the eye to the payoff.

---

## 3. Alternative cover concepts

**Option B — Context Flow Cover.**
A single horizontal flow as the hero image: *Notes → Local Markdown OS → Context Pack → AI tool → Feedback → Knowledge*, drawn as small labeled boxes with thin arrows on Paper. Title above, subtitle below. Emphasizes the *process* rather than the *structure*. Risk: a flow can get busy and lose legibility at thumbnail size.

**Option C — Local Memory Workbench.**
A calm "desk/workbench" composition: a few stacked Markdown document cards, one Project Map card, and one Context Pack card arranged like tools laid out on paper, with thin guide lines. Conveys "owned, tangible, local." Risk: more illustrative and harder to keep from feeling decorative or busy.

**Why Option A is the recommended first draft:**
It states the product most directly (structure → context), is the easiest to keep legible at thumbnail size, leans hardest into the "field manual" positioning, and reuses the existing repo visuals (folder tree, Context Pack) so production is fast and consistent. B and C are good later variations once A is stable.

## 4. Front cover copy

- **Title:** Build Your Local AI Knowledge OS
- **Subtitle:** A Practical System for Giving AI Tools Reliable Project Memory
- **Optional short positioning line:** Plain-text knowledge infrastructure for serious AI users.
- **Optional small bottom line:** `Local-first • Markdown-based • Tool-agnostic`

**What should NOT appear on the cover:**
- Author claims of guaranteed results, income, or "automation."
- "10x", "revolution", "empire", or hype words.
- Version numbers, dates, or edition noise in the main field.
- Long feature lists or bullet clutter.
- More than one tagline competing for attention.
- Tool logos or brand marks.

---

## 5. Visual elements

Include:
- **Folder tree** — simplified `10_AI_OS/` with the six core folders, in mono.
- **Markdown document icons** — simple page glyphs with a small `.md` or a tiny `#` mark; flat, no skeuomorphism.
- **Context Pack document** — one slightly emphasized page, the destination of the flow.
- **Thin arrows** — Line-gray or Soft amber, minimal, to show flow.
- **Light border lines** — subtle framing consistent with a manual.
- **Minimal labels** — only what's needed; mono for file/path text.

Exclude:
- **No screenshots** of any app or AI conversation.
- **No fake dashboards**, charts, or metrics.
- No photographic imagery; keep it diagrammatic and calm.

---

## 6. Back cover direction (paperback, even though Kindle ships first)

Keep it calm and honest, on the same Paper background.

- **Short book description** — 2–3 sentences: AI tools are powerful but forget project context; this book shows how to build a local, Markdown-based memory any AI can read, so you stop re-explaining your work.
- **Who it is for** — solo founders, AI consultants, indie hackers, technical writers, Markdown/Obsidian users, Claude/Codex/ChatGPT power users, and small teams needing reusable project memory.
- **What readers will build** — a working knowledge OS with Project Maps, Decision Records, Action Feedback, and Context Packs.
- **GitHub companion repo mention** — note the free public starter kit, templates, and safe examples (repository URL).
- **Privacy / local-first note** — your files stay on your machine; no account, no lock-in.
- **Simple diagram** — a small continuation of the folder tree or the memory loop, low contrast.
- **Do not include** exaggerated income or automation claims.

---

## 7. Kindle thumbnail requirements

- **Title must be readable small** — test at roughly 100–160 px wide.
- **Subtitle legible if possible** — if it can't stay crisp at thumbnail size, let the title dominate and accept softer subtitle.
- **Avoid dense diagrams** — simplify the folder tree for the thumbnail; detail can live in the full-size cover.
- **Strong contrast** — Graphite title on Paper background.
- **One main visual idea only** — structure-becomes-context; nothing competing.
- **Test at small size before finalizing** — shrink the export and check from arm's length.

---

## 8. What to avoid

- Robot heads, glowing brains, neon circuitry.
- Cyberpunk or dark "hacker" visuals.
- Fake dashboards, fake growth charts, money symbols.
- "10x" language; "AI automation empire" language.
- Dense enterprise architecture diagrams.
- "Second brain" cliché visuals.
- Too many AI tool logos (ideally none).
- Real private screenshots or any customer data.

## 10. Designer / Canva instructions

Practical steps to produce the first front-cover draft:

1. **Canvas ratio** — KDP eBook cover ~1.6:1 (e.g. 1600 × 2560 px). For paperback, generate the full wrap separately later using KDP's template for the chosen trim/page count.
2. **Safe margins** — keep all key elements ~10% in from each edge; nothing critical near the trim.
3. **Large title** — place *Build Your Local AI Knowledge OS* in the upper third, clean sans, Graphite on Paper, high contrast.
4. **Subtitle** — directly beneath, smaller; keep it on one or two lines.
5. **Simple diagram** — drop in the simplified folder-tree-to-Context-Pack visual in the middle; keep it light.
6. **Use the palette** — Paper background, Graphite text, restrained Ink blue / Local green / Soft amber accents, Line-gray connectors.
7. **Export PNG/JPG** for review at full size.
8. **Test thumbnail** — export a small version (~120 px wide) and confirm the title reads.
9. **Keep an editable source file** — keep the Figma/Canva project (and any SVGs) so revisions are easy.
10. **Typeset text manually** — never rely on AI-generated lettering for the final title/subtitle.
