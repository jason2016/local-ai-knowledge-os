# 02_Knowledge — Durable Notes

Knowledge holds the notes you want to **keep and reuse**: stable, distilled, trustworthy. If the Inbox is short-term memory and Distill is working memory, Knowledge is **long-term memory**.

These are the notes you (and your AI) will return to again and again.

## What belongs here

- Concepts and definitions explained in your own words
- How-to notes and repeatable procedures
- Reference material you rely on regularly
- Patterns, checklists, and rules of thumb
- Decision Records and Incident Records you want to keep accessible (see [`templates/`](../../../templates/))
- Reusable knowledge extracted from completed projects

## What does NOT belong here

- Unprocessed captures → [`00_INBOX/`](../00_INBOX/README.md)
- Work-in-progress thinking → [`01_Distill/`](../01_Distill/README.md)
- Live project status → [`06_Projects/`](../06_Projects/README.md)
- AI instructions and system prompts → [`09_AI_System/`](../09_AI_System/README.md)

## Conventions

- One topic per note. Keep notes atomic so they're easy to link and reuse.
- Use clear, descriptive filenames: `markdown-link-conventions.md`, not `note3.md`.
- Link related notes to each other so the AI can follow connections.
- Start each note with a one-line summary — it doubles as a great index entry.

## Why it matters for AI

Knowledge notes are the **grounding material** you hand to a model. When you build a Context Pack, you're mostly pulling from here. Well-written, atomic, linked notes make AI answers more accurate and more consistent across tools.
